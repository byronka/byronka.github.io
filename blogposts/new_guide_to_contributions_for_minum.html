New Guide to Contributions for Minum

September 8, 2024

I keep reading essays online (see notes below) how simplicity should be favored over complexity. That microservices and Kubernetes are overkill for most projects, all of which rings true with me. Why embrace undue complexity needlessly, why not keep things simple?

In 2018, I needed some software for demonstrating the industry's best practices for students. Our company's existing software was insufficient to teach from, which drove me to build a new project. This turned out to be an incredible opportunity to play with many of the prominent quality engineering ideas in the programming industry.

Around 2020, some colleagues and I wrote a timekeeping web application from scratch to play out these ideas in an experimental manner. We wondered how feasible it was to follow the spirit of the most ideal recommendations.

Afterwards, my friend Matt suggested:

Wouldn't it be nice for the common, generally useful stuff to be split out?

That program, called Minum, began development in December of 2021. Version 1 was released almost a year ago, and the project has entered a state of stability.

I have recently added a guide on making contributions to the project, if any of this piqued your interest. I would certainly value the insight of other practitioners.
